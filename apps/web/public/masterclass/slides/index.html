DAY 01 • CREDIT FUNDAMENTALS

Reading Your Credit Report Like a Pro

Personal Information

Verify your name, address, Social Security number, date of birth, and employment history. Errors here can cause mix-ups with other people's credit.

Name variations Address errors SSN mistakes

Accounts (Tradelines)

Review every credit card, loan, mortgage, and line of credit. Check account numbers, balances, credit limits, payment history, and account status.

Open accounts Closed accounts Payment history

Inquiries

Hard inquiries (from credit applications) affect your score for 12 months. Soft inquiries (pre-approvals, self-checks) don't impact your score.

Hard inquiries Soft inquiries

Public Records

Bankruptcies, tax liens, judgments, and foreclosures appear here. These severely damage your credit and can remain for 7-10 years.

Bankruptcies: 7-10 years

Tax liens: 7 years

Civil judgments: 7 years

Get Your Free Reports

You're entitled to one free report from each bureau every 12 months through AnnualCreditReport.com—the only official source.

The Three Bureaus:

• Equifax

• Experian

• TransUnion

DAY 01 • CREDIT FUNDAMENTALS

Finding Errors That Hurt Your Score

Identity Errors

Accounts that don't belong to you, wrong personal information, or mixed files with someone who has a similar name.

Red Flag: Accounts you never opened

Duplicate Accounts

The same debt listed multiple times, often when sold to collection agencies. This artificially inflates your debt burden.

Red Flag: Same account with different collectors

Status Errors

Accounts showing as open when closed, incorrect late payments, wrong balances, or incorrect credit limits.

Red Flag: Late payments you made on time

Outdated Information

Negative items that should have aged off your report after 7 years (10 for bankruptcies). Old addresses and employers.

Red Flag: 8+ year old collections

Balance Errors

Incorrect credit limits showing higher utilization, wrong account balances, or payments not properly credited.

Red Flag: Balance higher than your limit

Fraud Indicators

Accounts opened without your knowledge, inquiries you didn't authorize, or addresses where you've never lived.

Red Flag: Inquiries for credit you didn't apply for

Systematic Review Process

Review each bureau's report separately. Errors may appear on one but not others. Document every discrepancy with screenshots or printed pages.

DAY 02 • THE DISPUTE PROCESS

Your Rights Under the FCRA

Right to Access

You have the right to request and obtain all information about you in the files of consumer reporting agencies.

One free report every 12 months from each bureau

Right to Dispute

You can dispute incomplete or inaccurate information. The agency must investigate unless your dispute is frivolous.

Disputes must be investigated within 30 days

Right to Delete

Inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information must be removed or corrected within 30 days of your dispute.

Agencies must notify you of investigation results

Right to Sue

If a credit reporting agency violates your FCRA rights, you can sue in state or federal court for damages.

Willful Noncompliance

Actual damages, punitive damages, attorney fees, and costs

Negligent Noncompliance

Actual damages and attorney fees

Additional Rights

Right to know who requested your report

Right to opt-out of pre-screened offers

Right to seek damages from violators

Right to place security freezes

DAY 02 • THE DISPUTE PROCESS

FDCPA Protection Against Debt Collectors

What Collectors CANNOT Do

Call before 8 AM or after 9 PM

Call you at work if you tell them to stop

Use profane or abusive language

Threaten violence or legal action they can't take

Discuss your debt with third parties

Continue contact after receiving cease letter

What Collectors MUST Do

Send written validation notice within 5 days

Identify themselves as debt collectors

Provide debt amount and creditor name

Honor your request for verification

Debt Validation Process

Within 30 days of first contact, you can demand the collector prove the debt is yours and the amount is correct.

1. Request Validation

Send written request within 30 days

2. Collector Must Respond

Provide proof of debt ownership

3. Collection Must Cease

Until validation is provided

4. No Validation = No Collection

Cannot legally collect unvalidated debt

Cease & Desist Letters

You have the right to stop all communication from debt collectors by sending a cease and desist letter.

Important:

This doesn't eliminate the debt, but stops contact. Collectors can still sue you.

DAY 02 • THE DISPUTE PROCESS

The Complete Dispute Process

1

Document the Error

Take screenshots or print the page showing the error. Note the account number, creditor name, and exactly what's wrong. Gather supporting documents.

2

Write Your Dispute Letter

Clearly state what information you're disputing and why. Include copies (not originals) of supporting documents. Request deletion or correction.

3

Send Certified Mail with Return Receipt

Always send disputes via certified mail with return receipt requested. This creates proof of delivery and starts the 30-day clock.

4

Wait for Investigation

Credit bureaus have 30 days to investigate (45 if you provide additional information). They must forward your dispute to the data furnisher.

5

Review the Results

You'll receive results in writing. If verified, the item stays. If deleted or corrected, verify on your updated report. If unresolved, escalate.

Pro Tips

Dispute with all three bureaus separately

Keep copies of everything you send

Never dispute online—lose legal rights

Be specific about what's wrong

Dispute one error at a time per letter

Timeline

Dispute Sent Day 0
Investigation Complete Day 30
Results Received Day 35-40
Report Updated Day 45-60

DAY 03 • ADVANCED TACTICS

609 Letters: The Bureau's Verification Duty

What is Section 609?

Section 609 of the FCRA requires credit bureaus to disclose the sources of information on your credit report and maintain reasonable procedures to ensure maximum possible accuracy.

"The 609 letter forces bureaus to verify they have verifiable proof of the information they're reporting."

When to Use 609 Letters

After initial disputes come back "verified"

For old accounts where records may be lost

When you suspect the creditor lacks documentation

For collection accounts with multiple owners

What to Request

Original Signed Contract

The agreement with your signature

Complete Payment History

All payments and charges

Assignment of Debt

Proof collector owns the debt

Calculation of Balance

How they calculated what you owe

Important Notes

Bureaus don't have to provide documents, only verify they exist

If they can't verify, they must delete the item

Send to all three bureaus separately

DAY 03 • ADVANCED TACTICS

Validation Requests: Making Collectors Prove It

What is Debt Validation?

Debt validation is your right under the FDCPA to demand that a debt collector prove the debt is yours, the amount is correct, and they have the legal right to collect it.

You have 30 days from first contact to request validation

What Constitutes Proper Validation?

1

Original Contract

Signed agreement showing you owe the debt

2

Account History

Complete record of charges and payments

3

Assignment Documents

Proof the collector purchased the debt

4

License to Collect

Collector's authority in your state

The Validation Process

1. Send Validation Request

Within 30 days of first contact, send certified mail

2. Collection Must Cease

All collection activity stops until validation provided

3. Collector Responds (or Not)

They provide proof or fail to validate

4. If No Validation

They cannot legally collect and must delete from credit

Success Strategy

Most debt buyers purchase debts with minimal documentation. Many cannot provide proper validation, giving you leverage to have the item removed.

Key Point:

If they can't validate, they must cease collection AND remove from your credit report.

DAY 03 • ADVANCED TACTICS

Escalation Tactics for Non-Compliance

CFPB Complaints

File complaints with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau when bureaus or collectors violate your rights. The CFPB forwards complaints and requires responses.

consumerfinance.gov/complaint

State Attorney General

Your state AG's office handles consumer protection complaints. Many states have stronger laws than federal regulations, giving you additional leverage.

Search: "[Your State] Attorney General consumer complaint"

Small Claims Court

Sue for FCRA or FDCPA violations in small claims court. No attorney needed, filing fees are low ($50-200), and you can recover statutory damages plus actual damages.

Potential damages: $100-$1,000 per violation + actual damages

Consumer Attorney

Many consumer protection attorneys work on contingency for FCRA/FDCPA cases. They get paid from settlements or court-awarded fees, so no upfront cost to you.

Search: "Consumer protection attorney FCRA [Your City]"

BBB Complaints

File complaints with the Better Business Bureau. While not legally binding, BBB complaints can pressure companies to resolve issues to maintain ratings.

bbb.org

State Banking Department

File complaints with your state's banking or financial services department. They regulate credit reporting agencies and debt collectors operating in your state.

Search: "[Your State] banking department complaint"

DAY 04 • BUILDING POSITIVE CREDIT

Authorized User Strategy

How It Works

When someone adds you as an authorized user on their credit card, that card's entire payment history appears on your credit report—often instantly boosting your score.

You don't need to use the card or even possess it to benefit

What to Look For

Long History (5+ years)

Older accounts help your "length of credit history"

Perfect Payment History

No late payments ever—this is crucial

Low Utilization (Under 10%)

High balances can actually hurt your score

High Credit Limit

Helps your overall utilization ratio

Who to Ask

Family Members

Parents, siblings, grandparents with good credit

Close Friends

People who trust you and have excellent credit

Spouse/Partner

Joint financial goals make this natural

Important Considerations

Their negative activity hurts you too

You have no legal responsibility for the debt

Can be removed anytime by primary cardholder

Impact varies by credit scoring model

DAY 04 • BUILDING POSITIVE CREDIT

Secured Credit Cards That Build Credit

How Secured Cards Work

You provide a security deposit (typically $200-$500) which becomes your credit limit. The card works exactly like a regular credit card and reports to all three bureaus.

Your deposit is refunded when you close the account or graduate to unsecured

What to Look For

Reports to All Three Bureaus

Essential for building credit everywhere

No Annual Fee (or Low Fee)

Many good options have no annual fee

Graduation Path

Automatic upgrade to unsecured card

Low APR

Shop around for competitive rates

Best Practices

1. Use It Regularly

Small purchases monthly show activity

2. Pay in Full Every Month

Never carry a balance, avoid interest

3. Keep Utilization Under 10%

$20 balance on $200 limit is perfect

4. Set Up Autopay

Never miss a payment, ever

5. Wait for Graduation

Typically 6-12 months of good history

Top Secured Card Options

Discover it® Secured (cash back, graduates)

Capital One Platinum Secured (low deposit)

Citi® Secured Mastercard® (no annual fee)

OpenSky® Secured Visa® (no credit check)

DAY 04 • BUILDING POSITIVE CREDIT

Credit Builder Loans and Alternative Options

Credit Builder Loans

You make monthly payments into a locked savings account. The lender reports your payments as an installment loan. At the end, you get your money back plus interest.

Perfect for those with no credit or only credit cards

Rent Reporting Services

Services like RentTrack, LevelCredit, and Pinwheel report your rent payments to credit bureaus. Your largest monthly expense can now build your credit.

Reports to TransUnion and Equifax

Can add 24 months of past rent

Monthly fees: $6-10

Utility Bill Reporting

Experian Boost and similar services add utility, phone, and streaming payments to your credit report. Free services that can instantly boost your score.

Experian Boost: Free, instant boost

eCredable: Reports utilities to all bureaus

Only positive payments reported

Combination Strategy

The most effective approach combines multiple credit-building tools to create a diverse, positive credit profile quickly.

Month 1-3:

Secured card + authorized user

Month 4-6:

Add credit builder loan

Month 7+:

Add rent and utility reporting

DAY 05 • BUSINESS CREDIT & FUNDING

Business Entity Formation for Credit Building

Choose Your Business Structure

LLC (Limited Liability Company)

Most popular for small businesses. Separates personal and business liability. Pass-through taxation. Easy to maintain.

Corporation (S-Corp or C-Corp)

More formal structure. Better for raising capital. S-Corp avoids double taxation. More compliance requirements.

Foundation Requirements

1

EIN (Employer ID Number)

Free from IRS.gov—your business's Social Security number

2

Business Bank Account

Never mix personal and business finances

3

Business Address & Phone

Professional address (not PO box), dedicated phone

4

Business License

Required licenses for your industry and location

Business Credit Bureaus

Business credit is tracked separately from personal credit by different bureaus using different scoring models.

Dun & Bradstreet (DUNS)

PAYDEX score (1-100). Most widely used by vendors.

Experian Business

Intelliscore Plus (1-100). Credit risk assessment.

Equifax Business

Business Credit Risk Score. Payment behavior focus.

Key Differences

No legal right to free business credit reports

Scores range differently (typically 0-100)

Anyone can check your business credit

Higher credit limits available

Net 30 Accounts: Building Business Credit from Zero

What Are Net 30 Accounts?

Vendor accounts that give you 30 days to pay for purchases. They report your payment history to business credit bureaus, building your business credit profile.

Net 30 means payment due 30 days from invoice date

Starter Vendors (Easy Approval)

Uline

Shipping supplies, reports to D&B and Experian

Quill

Office supplies, reports to D&B

Grainger

Industrial supplies, reports to D&B

Summa Office Supplies

Office products, reports to all three bureaus

The Build Strategy

Month 1-2: Apply to 3-5 Starter Vendors

Make small purchases ($50-100), pay early

Month 3-4: First Trade Lines Report

D&B score starts building. Apply for more accounts.

Month 5-6: Add Gas & Store Cards

Shell, Chevron, Office Depot business cards

Amex, Chase, Capital One business cards
